

# Authorize the Zoom integration

After your Adobe Workfront administrator enables the Zoom integration, you need to authorize the connection between Workfront and your Zoom account. This is necessary for each Workfront user.

## Prerequisites

Your Workfront administrator must enable the Zoom integration before you can use it.

## Authorize the Zoom integration

<ol> 
 <li value="1"> <p>Go to a Workfront object such as a task or issue.</p> </li> 
 <li value="2">In the left panel, click<span class="bold"> Updates</span><img src="assets/comment-icon.png">.</li> 
 <li value="3"> <p>Click <span class="bold">Start a new update</span>, then click the camera icon that displays below the box.</p> <p> <img src="assets/zoom-updates-area-350x191.png" style="width: 350;height: 191;"> </p> </li> 
 <li value="4"> <p>In the Zoom screen that displays, sign in to your Zoom account.</p> <p> <img src="assets/zoom-sign-in-page-350x162.png" style="width: 350;height: 162;"> </p> <p>You can use your Zoom login credentials or any other method, such as signing in with Google.</p> <p>This authorization process occurs only one time, the first time you click this icon. The same is true for each user in your organization when they click the icon for the first time. </p> </li> 
 <li value="5"> <p>At the bottom of the page that says Workfront is requesting access to your Zoom account, click <span class="bold">Authorize</span>.</p> <p> <img src="assets/zoom-app-authorization-page-350x275.png" style="width: 350;height: 275;"> </p> <p>This page closes automatically when the connection is successfully authorized.</p> <p>You have connected Workfront to your Zoom account!</p> <p>Now you can click the camera icon <img src="assets/zoom-icon-updates.png"> in the Workfront Updates area whenever you want to insert a Zoom meeting URL into an update or comment in the Updates area.</p> <p> <img src="assets/zoom-insert-meeting-id-into-update-350x167.png" style="width: 350;height: 167;"> </p> </li> 
</ol>
